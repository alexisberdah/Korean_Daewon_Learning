## Grammar Point: Self-Introduction with 입니다/예요

### Structure: Subject + 입니다/이에요

**Form**: 
- Noun ending in vowel + **예요**
- Noun ending in consonant + **이에요**
- Formal (both): Noun + **입니다**

**Usage**: "To be" - stating identity, occupation, nationality

**Formation Rules**:
1. Check if noun ends in vowel or consonant
2. Add appropriate ending
3. 입니다 is more formal than 예요/이에요

**Examples**:

1. 저는 대원입니다.
   - Translation: I am Daewon.
   - Breakdown: 저는 (I-topic) + 대원 (Daewon) + 입니다 (am-formal)

2. 저는 학생이에요.
   - Translation: I am a student.
   - Breakdown: 저는 (I-topic) + 학생 (student-consonant ending) + 이에요 (am-polite)

3. 저는 선생님이에요.
   - Translation: I am a teacher.
   - Breakdown: 저는 (I-topic) + 선생님 (teacher-consonant) + 이에요 (am-polite)

**Common Mistakes**:
- ❌ 저는 학생예요 → ✓ 저는 학생이에요 (학생 ends in consonant!)
- ❌ 나는 대원입니다 → ✓ 저는 대원입니다 (use 저 for formal/polite)

**Practice Sentences**: (Fill in with 입니다, 예요, or 이에요)

**Answers**: 1. 입니다/예요 2. 입니다/이에요 3. 입니다/이에요 4. 입니다/이에요 5. 입니다/이에요

## Number Practice

### Counting 11-100

#### Native Korean (11-20)
- 열하나 (11) = 열 (10) + 하나 (1)
- 열둘 (12) = 열 (10) + 둘 (2)
- 열셋 (13), 열넷 (14), 열다섯 (15)
- 열여섯 (16), 열일곱 (17), 열여덟 (18)
- 열아홉 (19), 스물 (20)

#### Native Korean (20-100)
- 스물 (20), 서른 (30), 마흔 (40), 쉰 (50)
- 예순 (60), 일흔 (70), 여든 (80), 아흔 (90), 백 (100)

#### Sino-Korean (11-100)
- 십일 (11) = 십 (10) + 일 (1)
- 십이 (12), 십삼 (13), 십사 (14), 십오 (15)...
- 이십 (20) = 이 (2) + 십 (10)
- 삼십 (30), 사십 (40), 오십 (50), 육십 (60)
- 칠십 (70), 팔십 (80), 구십 (90), 백 (100)

### When to Use Which System?

**Native Korean Numbers (하나, 둘, 셋...)**:
- Age: 저는 스물여섯 살입니다 (I am 26 years old)
- Hours: 세 시 (3 o'clock)
- Counting items: 사과 두 개 (2 apples)
- *Note: Only goes up to 99*

**Sino-Korean Numbers (일, 이, 삼...)**:
- Dates: 삼월 오일 (March 5th)
- Money: 천 원 (1000 won)
- Phone numbers: 공일공-구팔칠육...
- Minutes: 이십오 분 (25 minutes)
- *Can go infinitely high*

## Speaking Practice

### Conversation Prompts (For Native Friend)
Practice these with your Korean friend today:

1. **Self-Introduction**:
   - Greet her
   - Introduce yourself (even though she knows you!)
   - Say nice to meet you
   - Let her correct your pronunciation

2. **Counting Practice**:
   - Count 1-10 in both systems
   - Ask her to count with you
   - Practice the tricky ones: 여섯, 여덟, 여든

3. **Number Quiz**:
   - She says a number in Korean (either system)
   - You say what number it is
   - Switch roles!

### Pronunciation Focus
Pay special attention to these sounds:
- **ㅓ vs ㅗ**: 여섯 (yeo-seot) vs 오 (o)
- **Final consonants**: 셋 (set), 넷 (net)
- **ㅆ (double s)**: 다섯 (da-seot)

## Cultural Note: Korean Number Systems

### Why Two Systems?
Korea adopted Chinese characters (한자/Hanja) historically, which brought Sino-Korean numbers. But native Korean numbers existed before. Today, both are used in different contexts.

### Fun Facts:
- Koreans can count to 99 in native Korean, but use Sino-Korean for 100+
- Phone numbers: Always Sino-Korean
- Ages: You can use both! 스물여섯 살 = 이십육 세
- In daily life, Koreans mix both systems naturally
